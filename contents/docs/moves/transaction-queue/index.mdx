---
title: Transaction queue
description: Review and sign prepared transactions
---

Every ask in LiSA is translated into concrete moves (swap, bridge, send, revoke), which resolve into the exact on-chain transactions required to fulfill it. Those transactions must run in a safe, deliberate order. The transaction queue is where they line up for your review and signature, turning intent into execution one confirmed step at a time.

### What it is
- A visible ordered list of unsigned transactions prepared from your request
- One intent can produce one transaction or a chain of dependent steps

### How it works
- LiSA sequences all required steps and places them in the queue
- You review and sign each item in order; nothing executes without a signature
- Steps on the same chain may be bundled when safe to reduce clicks

### Signatures and custody
- All transactions are unsigned until your wallet prompts you to sign on your device
- All signatures happen client side in your wallet
- No signature, no submission. No funds move without your approval

### After submission
- The next item becomes ready only after the previous one confirms on-chain
- You get explorer links and status updates for each submitted transaction
- If you stop signing, nothing else runs
